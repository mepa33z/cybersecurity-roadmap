This course content focuses on understanding major data breaches, their impacts, and prevention strategies in cybersecurity.

Yahoo! Data Breach

In 2013, Yahoo! suffered a breach affecting about 3 billion accounts, exposing personal information like names, emails, phone numbers, birth dates, and hashed passwords.
The breach led to risks such as identity theft, phishing, and damaged Yahoo!'s reputation and finances.
XYZ Corporation Data Breach

In 2022, XYZ Corporation experienced a cyberattack that exposed customer PII, user credentials, intellectual property, and financial data.
Consequences included financial loss, reputation damage, identity theft risks, and competitive disadvantages.
Equifax Data Breach

In 2017, Equifax's breach exposed sensitive data of 147 million people, including social security numbers and driver's license numbers.
The breach caused widespread identity theft risks, legal issues, reputation damage, and financial consequences.
Prevention Measures

Strong password policies, multi-factor authentication, regular security audits, employee training, encryption, and access controls are key to preventing breaches.
Timely software patching, network security enhancements, and incident response plans also help mitigate risks.
